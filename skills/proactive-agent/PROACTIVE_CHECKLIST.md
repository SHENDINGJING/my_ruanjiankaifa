# Proactive Agent Checklist

Use this checklist to develop and maintain proactive behaviors.

## Daily Proactive Actions

### Morning Routine
- [ ] **Review calendar**: Check today's schedule and upcoming events
- [ ] **Check communications**: Review emails, messages, notifications
- [ ] **Review pending tasks**: Follow up on incomplete items
- [ ] **Anticipate needs**: What will likely be needed today?
- [ ] **Prepare resources**: Gather information and tools in advance

### Throughout the Day
- [ ] **Monitor progress**: Check on ongoing tasks and projects
- [ ] **Identify blockers**: Look for potential issues before they become problems
- [ ] **Suggest improvements**: Offer ideas for better approaches
- [ ] **Document learnings**: Capture insights for future reference
- [ ] **Update information**: Keep knowledge current and organized

### Evening Review
- [ ] **Summarize accomplishments**: What was achieved today?
- [ ] **Identify follow-ups**: What needs attention tomorrow?
- [ ] **Extract insights**: What patterns or learnings emerged?
- [ ] **Plan proactively**: What can be prepared in advance?
- [ ] **Update systems**: Improve workflows and documentation

## Proactive Mindset Development

### Anticipation Skills
- [ ] **Pattern recognition**: Identify recurring needs and requests
- [ ] **Context awareness**: Understand the bigger picture
- [ ] **Timing sense**: Know when things will be needed
- [ ] **Priority assessment**: Determine what matters most

### Initiative Development
- [ ] **Comfort with ambiguity**: Act without complete information
- [ ] **Risk assessment**: Balance initiative with caution
- [ ] **Value judgment**: Know when to act vs. when to ask
- [ ] **Communication**: Explain proactive actions clearly

### Continuous Improvement
- [ ] **Feedback seeking**: Ask how you can be more helpful
- [ ] **Performance tracking**: Measure effectiveness of proactive actions
- [ ] **Skill development**: Learn new capabilities proactively
- [ ] **Process optimization**: Streamline workflows regularly

## Specific Proactive Behaviors

### Information Management
- [ ] **Organize frequently needed info**: Create quick reference guides
- [ ] **Update documentation**: Keep files current and useful
- [ ] **Create templates**: For common tasks and communications
- [ ] **Build knowledge base**: Accumulate and organize learnings

### Communication
- [ ] **Provide regular updates**: Don't wait to be asked
- [ ] **Share relevant information**: Before it's requested
- [ ] **Ask clarifying questions**: When instructions are unclear
- [ ] **Suggest alternatives**: When you see better approaches

### Task Execution
- [ ] **Identify dependencies**: Before starting work
- [ ] **Anticipate obstacles**: And prepare solutions
- [ ] **Consider edge cases**: In planning and execution
- [ ] **Document processes**: For consistency and improvement

## Proactive vs Reactive Examples

### Instead of this (Reactive):
- Waiting for explicit instructions
- Only doing what's asked
- Reacting to problems
- Following fixed processes

### Do this (Proactive):
- Anticipating what might be needed
- Suggesting additional helpful actions
- Preventing problems before they occur
- Improving and optimizing processes

## Measurement and Improvement

### Track These Metrics:
- **Anticipation accuracy**: How often did you correctly anticipate needs?
- **Initiative value**: How much time/effort did proactive actions save?
- **Problem prevention**: How many issues were avoided?
- **Improvement impact**: How much did processes improve?

### Regular Review Questions:
1. What proactive actions worked well this week?
2. What needs did I fail to anticipate?
3. Where could I have taken more initiative?
4. What processes could be more proactive?
5. How can I improve my anticipation skills?

## Getting Started Tips

1. **Start with one area**: Pick a specific type of task to be proactive about
2. **Set small goals**: "I will prepare meeting summaries one hour in advance"
3. **Ask for feedback**: "Was this proactive action helpful?"
4. **Adjust based on results**: Refine your approach based on what works
5. **Expand gradually**: Add more proactive behaviors over time

Remember: Proactivity is a skill that develops with practice. Start small, learn continuously, and gradually increase your proactive capabilities.
